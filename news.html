About Lab

Our lab is interested in linking cell biology of the neuron to function and behaviour of the organism. We focus on how transport of axonal cargo is regulated, using C. elegans as a model organism. Using a combination of classical genetics, molecular and biochemical approaches, along with in vivo imaging techniques, our lab investigates synaptic vesicle protein trafficking, regulation of cargo-motor complex, axoplasmic crowding and regulation of cargo distribution (viz. synaptic vesicles and mitochondria) along the neuronal process.

Research Summary

Regulation of pre-synaptic vesicle transport.

Regulation of mitochondrial transport and distributions in neurons.

Developing new in vivo tools to help us both disrupt and image cargo transport in neurons.

Quantitative understanding of pre-SV transport.
